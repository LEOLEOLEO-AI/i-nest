## Abstract

ABSTRACT In contemporary times, content-based image retrieval (CBIR) techniques have gained widespread acceptance as a means for end-users to discern and extract specific image content from vast repositories. However, it is noteworthy that a substantial majority of CBIR studies continue to rely on linear methodologies such as gradient-based and derivative-based edge detection techniques. This research explores the integration of bioinspired Spiking Neural Network (SNN) based edge detection within CBIR. We introduce an innovative, computationally efficient SNN-based approach designed explicitly for CBIR applications, outperforming existing SNN models by reducing computational overhead by 2.5 times.
