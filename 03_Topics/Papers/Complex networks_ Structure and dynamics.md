This foundational 2006 paper provides a comprehensive survey of the structural properties and dynamic behaviors of complex networks, establishing core principles for understanding how network topology influences emergent phenomena. It synthesizes key concepts like scale-free networks, small-world properties, and network robustness that form the theoretical basis for studying intelligence emergence in physical systems.

## Key Findings
- Scale-free networks with power-law degree distributions are ubiquitous in real-world systems
- Small-world networks exhibit high clustering with short path lengths enabling efficient information flow
- Network topology fundamentally determines system robustness, vulnerability, and dynamic behavior
- Modular structure and hierarchical organization are common features of complex networks
- Dynamic processes on networks (like synchronization) depend critically on structural properties
