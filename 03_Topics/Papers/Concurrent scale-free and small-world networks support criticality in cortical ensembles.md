## Core Contribution
Demonstrates that specific complex network topologies (concurrent scale-free and small-world) support the emergence of criticality in neuronal networks, linking structural connectivity to optimal functional dynamics like neuronal avalanches.

## Next Steps for iNEST
Integrate experimental topology measurements (e.g., via imaging or dense electrode arrays) with computational models. Explore dynamic, plastic networks that self-organize toward critical topologies. Extend to task-dependent activity and learning paradigms.

## Key Findings
- Concurrent scale-free and small-world topologies best support criticality, optimizing functional properties like information capacity and dynamic range.
- Scale-free networks alone promote criticality but with reduced dynamic range; small-world networks enhance synchronization but may lead to supercritical states.
- Random networks typically result in subcritical dynamics, lacking optimal functional features.
- Critical states emerge from specific topological interplay, not from single topology types.

## Research Gaps
- Mechanisms linking specific topologies to criticality in biological networks remain unclear.
- Lack of real-time topological data from experimental preparations to validate models.
- Role of plasticity and adaptive rewiring in maintaining critical states not addressed.
- How criticality translates to higher-order cognitive functions or learning.
