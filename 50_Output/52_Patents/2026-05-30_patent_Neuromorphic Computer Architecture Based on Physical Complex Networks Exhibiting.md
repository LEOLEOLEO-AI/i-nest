Computer Architecture, Neuromorphic Engineering, Complex Systems

## 核心创新
A neuromorphic computer hardware architecture that physically instantiates a complex network of computational nodes (e.g., via chiplet-based design) engineered to operate at a dynamical critical point, where the system exhibits optimal information processing, memory, and emergent collective computational properties akin to biological neural systems.

## 与现有技术区别
Existing neuromorphic chips often emulate spiking neurons with fixed or simple adaptive connectivity. This invention differs by architecting the physical hardware substrate itself as a reconfigurable complex network whose global dynamical state (e.g., near a phase transition/criticality) is actively maintained to foster emergent computational capabilities, moving beyond pre-defined neural network models to hardware that inherently supports adaptive, collective computation.

## 可用仿真工具

- In-situ sensors for monitoring network criticality metrics

## 权利要求草案
1. 1. A neuromorphic computer comprising: a plurality of processing chiplets interconnected in a reconfigurable physical network topology; a global controller configured to adjust interconnection parameters among the chiplets to maintain the network's dynamical state within a predefined regime of criticality, as measured by one or more statistical metrics of network activity.
2. 2. The neuromorphic computer of claim 1, wherein the regime of criticality is characterized by scale-free correlations in activity propagation and maximizes a balance between information transmission and storage within the chiplet network.
3. 3. A method for computation using the neuromorphic computer of claim 1, comprising: mapping a computational problem onto an initial state of the chiplet network; allowing the network to evolve dynamically while maintained near criticality; and reading out a resultant stable pattern or trajectory from the network as a solution to the problem.
