# Cover Letter

**To:** Editor-in-Chief, *Engineering*  
**Special Issue:** Sustainable Intelligent Computing  
**Date:** June 17, 2026

Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our review manuscript entitled **"From von Neumann to Network-Centric: A Scoping Review of the Computing Paradigm Migration toward Sustainable Intelligent Computing"** for consideration in the *Engineering* Special Issue on Sustainable Intelligent Computing.

**Relevance to the Special Issue.** The sustainability crisis in computing is fundamentally an energy problem. Our review establishes, through systematic analysis of four canonical computing scenarios (general-purpose, intelligent, high-performance, and signal processing), that data movement — not computation — accounts for 60-90% of total energy consumption, and this fraction is increasing at every process generation. The sustainable path forward lies not in marginally more efficient processors, but in fundamentally rethinking the computation-communication relationship through software-defined interconnect and topology-centric computing. This directly addresses the special issue's core theme of reconciling computational capability with environmental sustainability.

**Key Contributions.**
1. Formalization of the Data-Movement Dominance Law with multi-scenario empirical validation.
2. Demonstration that hardware operators converge to <=10 primitives, making operator optimization a diminishing-returns strategy.
3. Introduction of eleven spanning data-movement meta-primitives with a formal cost model.
4. Proposal of the liquid unified architecture integrating six non-von-Neumann pathways.
5. Formalization of topology-centric computing (TCC) as a mathematical framework with SDI as a necessary enabler.
