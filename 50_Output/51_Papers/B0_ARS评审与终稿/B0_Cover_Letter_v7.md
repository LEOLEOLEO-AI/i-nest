## Why This Paper Fits the Special Issue

The special issue calls for research addressing the sustainability of intelligent computing. Our paper directly addresses this by demonstrating that **data movement, not computation, is the fundamental sustainability bottleneck** — consuming ~90% of energy in modern AI workloads. We propose a concrete architectural path forward: Software-Defined Interconnect (SDI) and the Liquid Unified Architecture.

## What This Paper Contributes

1. **Data-Movement Dominance Law:** A formal, falsifiable relationship showing that the energy fraction of data movement monotonically increases with process scaling
2. **First-principles decomposition:** All computation reducible to ≤10 operators + 11 data-movement meta-primitives with formal cost model
3. **SDI architecture:** Runtime-programmable topology with quantified benefit thresholds
4. **Liquid Unified Architecture:** Integrates six non-von-Neumann pathways in a single reconfigurable framework
5. **Five testable research agendas** for empirical validation
