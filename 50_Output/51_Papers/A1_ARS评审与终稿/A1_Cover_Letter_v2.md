## Significance

This paper presents the **Coordination Spatiotemporal Complexity (CST) theorem** — a quantitative, first-principles framework that unifies intelligence emergence across biological and artificial systems. The CST formula, CST = (S_c · T_c) · exp(α · Γ_st), derives six universal intelligence thresholds at natural constants {1/√2, 1, φ, e, π, δ} and is validated across 40 systems spanning 8 biological taxonomic grades and 18 distinct ANN/NMH architectural families (Spearman ρ = 0.976).

Key findings include:
1. All binary-digital ANNs (including GPT-4, Llama-3, DeepSeek-V3) remain below CST ≈ 0.35 — a theoretically derived ceiling confirming the "α-barrier" prediction
2. Neuromorphic hardware (Intel Loihi-2, CST = 0.7816) is separately classified, confirming the α-barrier framework
3. Intelligence Efficiency η_I reveals an approximately six-order-of-magnitude gap between biological brains and current AI
4. A four-generation hardware roadmap identifies the physically necessary path toward general intelligence

## Fit with Journal Scope

This work bridges statistical physics (phase transitions, renormalization group theory), complex network science, and artificial intelligence — core interests of *Nature Physics*. The derivation of universal constants from first principles and the cross-domain validation make this suitable for a broad interdisciplinary audience.
