# Cover Letter

**To:** Editor-in-Chief, *Nature Physics*  
**Date:** June 19, 2026

Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled **"From Compute to Complexity: A Physical Theory of Intelligence Emergence and Its Implications for Artificial General Intelligence"** for consideration in *Nature Physics*.

**The problem.** Modern AI development is defined by a single operating principle: scale compute, and intelligence will follow. Yet empirical scaling laws now reveal that capability improvements per unit energy follow a sub-linear curve — each successive generation buys less intelligence per joule. We argue this is because the field has been measuring the wrong quantity: functional benchmark performance rather than emergent intelligence potential.

**Our contribution.** We propose the Coordination Spatiotemporal Complexity (CST) theorem — CST = (S_c · T_c) · e^(alpha · Gamma_st) — a physical framework in which structural integration, dynamical richness, and their physical coupling jointly determine emergent intelligence potential. The exponential coupling term is derived from the non-Abelian gauge structure of network state space, where alpha = ln(M_eff) encodes the device-specific number of distinguishable states per node. Six intelligence thresholds {1/sqrt(2), 1, phi, e, pi, delta} emerge analytically from the GL(k, R) symmetry-breaking cascade.

**Key findings:**
1. CST validated across 40 biological and artificial systems (Spearman rho = 0.976, eight taxonomic grades)
2. All binary-digital architectures are permanently confined below the first emergence threshold (CST < 0.707), regardless of parameter count
3. Biological systems span five orders of magnitude in Intelligence Efficiency
4. A four-generation hardware roadmap identifies the physically necessary path from present systems to general intelligence
5. SDI topology simulations confirm superlinear structured-efficiency scaling (27x at N=1024)
6. Multi-scale experiments demonstrate spontaneous emergence of phototaxis, chemotaxis, and pattern memory with no explicit supervision

**Significance.** This work establishes the first quantitative, first-principles framework connecting network topology, temporal dynamics, and physical substrate to emergent intelligence — resolving von Neumann's 1948 qualitative conjecture into a testable physical theory with precise numerical thresholds.
