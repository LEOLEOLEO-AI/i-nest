## 摘要

The complexity of photonic designs is rapidly increasing as the integration of more devices and materials creates stringent geometric constraints, material-dependent loss characteristics, and vertical stack dependent constraints. Consequently, optimizing the routing of waveguides and metal interconnects is essential to minimize material footprint, reduce optical losses, and improve power efficiency. Traditional manual routing methods are not only time-consuming but also impractical for managing multiple design variables simultaneously. To address these challenges, we introduce an automated routing tool that leverages graph search algorithms to route both metal wires and waveguides effectively. Key algorithmic innovations include adaptive path correction, obstacle-aware routing, and heuristic-based optimization that dynamically adjusts interconnects to adhere to design constraints and efficiency considerations. To increase efficiency and continuously optimize routing in a holistic approach, we introduce iterative optimization processes that enable automatic optimization and rapid design exploration.
