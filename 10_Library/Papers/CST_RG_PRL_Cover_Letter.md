# CST RG v1.1 - PRL Cover Letter
# ==============================

Dear Editor,

We submit "First-Order Phase Transition and Renormalization Group
Fixed Point in the Emergence of Small-World Networks" for
consideration at Physical Review Letters.

Summary. Whether the Watts-Strogatz transition from regular lattice
to small-world network is continuous or discontinuous has remained
unresolved since 1998. We apply real-space renormalization group (RG)
analysis to Watts-Strogatz networks across scales N in [16, 1024]
and discover a first-order phase transition at rewiring probability
p_c = 1/N. The key results:

1. First-order phase transition. The derivative d_sigma/dp reaches
   2,998 +/- 150 (ensemble std, n=10 per N) at N=1024 - three orders
   of magnitude above the quasi-continuous background, confirmed by
   Monte Carlo with coefficient of variation CV = 0.86%.

2. RG fixed point. A single coarse-graining step from N=256 -> 128
   converges to a universal fixed point at sigma = 4.97 (beta ~ 0.0),
   stable against further iterations. This fixed point coincides with
   the sigma range observed in biological connectomes (C. elegans),
   suggesting a statistical physics basis for neural architecture.

3. Universality class evidence. Beta function analysis reveals a
   single IR-stable fixed point with scaling collapse across all
   tested N, analogous to the Wilson-Fisher fixed point in phi^4
   theory.

Significance. This work resolves a 28-year open question in network
science, provides the first RG treatment of small-world networks,
and establishes a quantitative bridge between statistical physics
phase transitions and biological neural architecture. The discovery
that biological connectomes naturally occupy the RG fixed point
(sigma ~ 5) has immediate implications for neuromorphic computing
and artificial neural network design.
