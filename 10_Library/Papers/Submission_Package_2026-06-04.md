Dear Editor,

We submit "From Compute to Complexity: A Physical Theory of Intelligence
Emergence and Its Implications for Artificial General Intelligence" for
consideration at Nature Machine Intelligence.

**Summary.** The current AI scaling paradigm — more parameters, more
data, more compute — faces a thermodynamic asymptote: each successive
generation buys less intelligence per joule. We argue this is not an
engineering deficiency but a consequence of pursuing the wrong variable.
We propose the Coordination Spatiotemporal Complexity (CST) theorem:
CST = (S_c * T_c) * exp(alpha * Gamma_st), where structural integration,
dynamical richness, and their physical coupling jointly determine
emergent intelligence potential.

**Key findings:**
1. Six universal intelligence thresholds derived at natural constants
   {1/sqrt(2), 1, phi, e, pi, delta=4.669}, validated across 40 systems
   spanning 8 taxonomic grades (Spearman rho=0.976).
2. All binary-digital AI systems (GPT-2 to MoE) remain below Level I
   (perception), bottlenecked by the binary 伪=0.69 ceiling — a
   structural lock that parameter scaling cannot break.
3. Four-generation hardware roadmap from binary-digital (Gen0) to
   memristive-SDI (Gen3), with Intel Loihi-2 confirming Gen1 viability.
4. SDI topology simulations: (a) emergence threshold N=48, superlinear
   S_eff/Rand growth to 26.8x at N=1024, first-order phase transition
   at d_sigma/dp=2,998; (b) cross-species connectome validation: C. elegans
   convergence to sigma=5.04 within CST-predicted optimal range; (c) DVSGesture
   multi-frame temporal validation: +13.0% gain confirming Tc non-compressibility;
   (d) LNN FEP+STDP achieving 80.1% accuracy — within 1.4% of LSTM baseline.

**Significance.** This work provides the first quantitative bridge from
topological complexity to measurable intelligence, grounded in
statistical physics. It explains why current AI cannot achieve general
intelligence and what physical substrate properties are required.
The framework is falsifiable (see Methods Section 4) and has immediate
engineering implications for next-generation computing architectures.

**Prior publication.** A preprint (v5.1) was posted on arXiv in August
2025. The current manuscript (v27-FINAL) incorporates 40-system
validation, neuromorphic hardware results, and SDI computational
verification not present in earlier versions.

We confirm no competing interests and that all authors have approved
the manuscript.

Sincerely,
Qinrang Liu

**Significance.** This provides the first rigorous mathematical
framework connecting topology generation to physical computation,
with direct engineering implications for wafer-scale chiplet
