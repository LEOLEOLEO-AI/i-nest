# 💡 Statistical mechanics of complex networks

**Real-world networks universally exhibit small-world and scale-free topologies that arise from growth and preferential attachment, suggesting that intelligent physical networks may similarly self-organize into efficient, robust architectures.**

## 与研究主线关联
线程: **复杂网络拓扑与计算**

The paper demonstrates that complex network topologies emerge from simple statistical mechanics principles. For iNEST, this suggests that physical neural networks (e.g., wafer-scale SNNs or reservoir computers) could be grown using local rules like preferential attachment to naturally achieve optimal connectivity for computation. This could lead to self-organizing hardware that balances efficiency and robustness without explicit top-down design.
