### SDI Simulations

#### C. elegans (302 neurons)
- V30 result: σ=6.796, α=3.556, C=0.390, L=2.662 (11.86s)
- Full plasticity: E-L ratio=19.6%, STDP enabled, rebuild every 10 steps

#### Drosophila Hemibrain (21,739 neurons)
- V30 result: σ=62.918, α=128.51, C=0.6, L=1.390 (161.4s, 300 steps)
- ⚠️ **CRITICAL ISSUE**: sigma overestimated by ~10x (C_est=0.6 vs realistic ~0.04-0.08)
- Root cause: `_compute_sigma_fast()` uses naive formula: C_est = min(0.6, deg.mean * p * 5)
  - For Hemibrain: deg.mean~14.2, p_er~1.2e-5, gives C_est=0.6 (capped), but real C≈0.04-0.08
  - L also unreliable: deg.mean * p_er * N = 14.2 * 1.2e-5 * 21739 ≈ 3.7 >> realistic path length
- Alpha=128.51: cascades cover 86% of neurons (model designed for small cascades)
- E-L ratio=0% (apply_rules skipped for N>10000 — no plasticity on real topology)

#### V30 Key Decisions
- Single universal V30 preferred (multi-species via SPECIES_CONFIG)
- Hemibrain: vectorized STDP + skip rebuild for N > 10000 (avoid hanging)
- All expensive ops (stdp loop, rebuild, apply_rules) skipped for N > 10000
- Fast sigma formula breaks for large sparse networks
