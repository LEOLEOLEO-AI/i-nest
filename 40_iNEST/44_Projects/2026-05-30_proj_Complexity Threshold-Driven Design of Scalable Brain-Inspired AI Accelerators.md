## 背景
The mesoscopic complexity threshold theory posits that brain-like intelligence emerges only when system complexity exceeds a critical point, which is unattainable in conventional monolithic chips. The SDSoW approach allows integration of thousands of chiplets, crossing this threshold. This project will develop a design methodology and a prototype accelerator that leverages complexity scaling to achieve advanced cognitive capabilities such as one-shot learning and causal reasoning on-chip.

## 目标
['Formalize the complexity threshold for spiking neural networks in terms of synaptic count, connectivity, and plasticity rules.', 'Design a chiplet-based accelerator architecture that scales to >1 million neurons with on-chip learning, using SDSoW.', 'Fabricate a 300-mm wafer-scale prototype with 64 chiplets, each containing 16k neurons, interconnected via a 2D mesh.', 'Demonstrate emergent few-shot learning and simple reasoning tasks on the prototype, with power efficiency <1 pJ/synaptic operation.']

## 时间线
{'Q1': 'Theoretical modeling and simulation of complexity scaling; chiplet microarchitecture design (neuron core, synapse array, learning engine).', 'Q2': 'RTL design and verification; tape-out of single chiplet; wafer-scale network-on-wafer design; begin software toolchain.', 'Q3': 'Chiplet testing and characterization; wafer assembly (64 chiplets on interposer); bring-up and basic functionality tests.', 'Q4': 'Complexity scaling experiments (varying active chiplets); benchmark on few-shot learning (Omniglot) and causal reasoning tasks; final reporting.'}

## 里程碑
- M1 (Q1): Simulation shows clear phase transition in learning capability at ~500k synapses.
- M2 (Q2): Chiplet tape-out with DRC/LVS clean.
- M3 (Q3): 64-chiplet system operational; 1M-neuron SNN mapped and running at real-time.
- M4 (Q4): Few-shot classification accuracy >85% on 5-way 1-shot Omniglot, with energy <100 µJ per inference.
