## Overview
Topological Centric Computing (TCC) is an emerging paradigm that reimagines system architecture by placing the topology of interconnects—between compute, memory, and storage—at the center of design, moving beyond traditional bus- or mesh-based approaches. Current research is heavily focused on integrating photonic interconnects (CPO/OCS), chiplet-based modular architectures (Simba), and software-defined systems (SDSoW) to achieve system-level superlinear performance gains. The field is rapidly converging on the need for new metrics and design tools to evaluate and optimize these complex networks.

- **Topology-Aware AI Accelerators**: Moving beyond Simba’s chiplet mesh, next-generation designs will likely incorporate reconfigurable photonic crossbars and non-blocking optical topologies directly into the compute fabric, blurring the line between network and processor.
