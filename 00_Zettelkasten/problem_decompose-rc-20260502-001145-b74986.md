## Sub-questions

### SQ-1 · Formal Algebraic Completeness and Minimality of Collective Communication-Computation Primitive Sets

> **Core Question:** Does any prior work formally characterize a named set of distributed communication-computation primitives — analogous to functional completeness in Boolean logic or Turing completeness in computation theory — over the space of patterns arising in AI training/inference, FFT pipelines, or radar signal processing? Specifically, has any prior work proven or disproven the existence of an irreducible basis within a set equivalent to or containing {AllReduce, AllGather, Broadcast, AlltoAll, GEMM, Scan, Map, Reduce}?

**Decomposed Search Targets:**

- **SQ-1a — MPI and Collective Taxonomy Literature:** What algebraic or compositional relationships between MPI-4 collectives (AllReduce, Reduce-Scatter, AllGather, Barrier, Scan, Exscan) have been formally characterized? Does MPI Forum or associated academic work treat any subset as a *basis* from which others are derivable, or is the set defined purely by operational semantics?
  - *Key terms:* `MPI collective taxonomy`, `collective derivability`, `reduce-scatter allgather decomposition`, `MPI-4 formal semantics`
  - *Expected gap:* MPI collectives are defined by behavioral contracts, not by algebraic minimality; no completeness proof is expected

- **SQ-1b — NCCL, OneCCL, and Collective Library Design Rationale:** Do NCCL (NVIDIA), OneCCL (Intel), or RCCL (AMD) documentation or associated papers justify their primitive selection on formal completeness grounds, or is selection empirically driven by hardware topology and bandwidth utilization?
  - *Key terms:* `NCCL primitive selection`, `OneCCL collective algebra`, `ring allreduce derivation`, `collective library design rationale`
  - *Expected gap:* Empirical/performance-driven selection; no formal basis claimed

- **SQ-1c — Algebraic Models of Parallel Computation (BSP, LogP, CGM):** Do Bulk Synchronous Parallel (BSP), LogP, or Coarse-Grained Multicomputer (CGM) models provide a completeness-theoretic account of communication patterns? Does the "scan" primitive appear as a generator in any algebraic treatment of parallel prefix computation?
  - *Key terms:* `BSP collective completeness`, `parallel prefix algebra`, `scan as universal primitive`, `CGM communication primitives`
  - *Expected gap:* BSP/LogP treat communication cost, not algebraic completeness; scan universality is known for sequential prefix but not for distributed collective basis

- **SQ-1d — GEMM and Map/Reduce as Communication-Computation Hybrids:** Has prior work unified linear-algebraic primitives (GEMM) with communication collectives (AllReduce, AlltoAll) under a single algebraic framework? Does any work treat GEMM as a collective primitive rather than a local compute primitive?
  - *Key terms:* `distributed GEMM collective`, `linear algebra collective unification`, `BLAS communication model`, `Cannon algorithm collective decomposition`
  - *Expected gap:* GEMM is uniformly treated as local compute; its unification with communication collectives in a single primitive algebra is not expected to exist
